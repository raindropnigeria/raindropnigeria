  • What is Raindrop?

    Raindrop is a logistics and supply chain company focused on agriculture. We build the storage, distribution, and transportation systems that help farmers and businesses move food efficiently.

  • Why is Raindrop important for farmers?

    Because most farmers struggle with storage and moving their goods. Raindrop gives them the infrastructure to store in bulk, keep products fresh, and reach markets on time.

  • How does Raindrop support businesses?

    We make it easy for food platforms, retailers, and restaurants to access farm products without worrying about logistics. Businesses can rely on our supply chain to get goods delivered smoothly and in good condition.

  • Do you only work with big companies?

    No. Raindrop supports both small farmers and large businesses. Our goal is to connect everyone in the food supply chain — from individual farmers to big distributors.

  • What problems is Raindrop solving in Nigeria?

    Poor storage, bad transportation networks, and broken supply chains. We're building the backbone that makes agriculture work at scale.

  • Is Raindrop only for agriculture?

    Our main focus is agriculture, but the systems we build — storage, logistics, and supply chain — can also support other industries over time.

  • Where does Raindrop operate?

    We are starting in Nigeria but our vision is bigger — to expand across Africa and eventually beyond, creating a stronger food supply chain wherever it's needed.

  • How does Raindrop make money?

    We provide logistics, storage, and supply services to farmers, businesses, and food platforms. They rely on us to move and store their goods, and that's how we earn.

  • What makes Raindrop different?

    We're not just a delivery company. We're building the missing infrastructure — the invisible systems that connect farmers to businesses and consumers, creating long-term change.

  • What's the future vision of Raindrop?

    To be the backbone of agriculture in Nigeria and Africa. With Raindrop's infrastructure in place, countless businesses can grow on top of it, just like technology companies grew on top of the internet.
